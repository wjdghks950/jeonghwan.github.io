About

Currently working as a research associate at IR&NLP Lab at KAIST. I obtained my Master's from the School of Computing, KAIST, where I was advised by Prof. Sung-Hyon Myaeng. My primary research interests lie in the intersection of natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning. I aspire to build a general-purpose educational system that overcomes the boundaries of human teachers and disseminates knowledge worldwide. I strongly believe that my research in NLP is going to contribute to the development of such a system, and I will orient my research towards accomplishing the goal by solving untackled challenges in these fields. To this end, I am interested in understanding barriers preventing NLP systems from being practical, finding hidden assumptions that are favorable to academic settings, and solving them through novel machine learning approaches. In particular, my research aims to i) make NLP systems cheaper (i.e., efficient) in terms of data and computational resources, and ii) design robust and reliable models to produce desirable outcomes in the wild. Here are some keywords:

  • Question Answering
  • Information Retrieval
  • Efficiency
  • Robustness
I will be starting my Ph.D. at Georgia Tech in Fall 2022!
