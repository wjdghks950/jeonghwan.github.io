About

Ph.D. student at BLENDER Lab at University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), currently advised by Professor Heng Ji

I obtained my Master's from the School of Computing, KAIST, where I was advised by Prof. Sung-Hyon Myaeng.

My primary research interests lie in the intersection of natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning. As an NLP researcher, I want to build next-generation language models that are capable of dealing with the interplay of general and domain-specific knowledge; these models, hopefully, will serve to empower individuals in their daily effort to acquire new knowledge.

I'm particularly interested in developing neuro-symbolic language models that can process symbols that possess certain symbol-specific characteristics (e.g., numbers, graphs) and understand those symbols in the light of peripheral context and external knowledge.

Here are some keywords about my research interests:

  • Question Answering (QA)
  • Language Modeling
  • Retrieval-Augmented LMs
  • Neuro-symbolic LMs
